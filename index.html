Price Trends

Groceries and daily essentials go through price fluctuations every now and then. This project uses visualizations to speak about insights from historical data on retail prices of daily essentials including milk, meat, towels and more. There are a few interesting facts and hidden insights on price trends of consumer goods, hidden behind the vast data. A comparison of the general inflation rate with product price trends to find unusual behaviors is also present. The seasonal variation in price of farm-produces, the dependency of bread on wheat, and a tool to calculate the financial damage smoking causes is all out there in the form of charts and dashboards. A custom-build helper is present for each of the visualizations to help user onboard and read the insights easily. Most of the visualizations make use of mouse hover to drive users interest to specific areas, and highlight interesting components.

Seasonal trends

On the graphic, we can observe that multiple products follow a seasonal trend. Preselected products are shown in the combobox. They are sorted by the one with the most visible seasonal trends to those with smaller seasonal trends. With this, we can see that some products depends of the season of each product production. It is the case for carrots which have their lower price in september and their higher price in june. The same applies for onions and oranges (not sure oranges). The wheat harvest season starting mid july/august, it corresponds to the point where less flour is available and so the price increase

Product outliers: Products with the biggest and smallest price variation

The graph opposite shows the products that had the biggest price changes according to the period chosen. The three products with the largest price change and the three products with the smallest price change. Use the controls to play with the visualization. You will see, for example, that between September 1996 and November 2001, the three products with the biggest price changes were cigarettes, rib roast premium and sirloin steak. Those with the smallest price change are macaroni, toothpaste and tomato juice. The cigarette stands out by having had, in all periods of time, the biggest price increase.

Inflation rate compared to product prices growth rate

On the following graphic we can see all products plotted for the full period. We can see in green the top 6 product where the price growth rate deviates multiple times from the inflation curve. This products are onions, carrots, potatoes, roasted coffee, peanut butter and canned salmon.

Similar trend

We can see that multiple types of products share similar trends. We can classify these groups of products of similar trends in 3 categories:

  • Products of similar food categories with similar trends
  • Products of similar food categories with different trends
  • Products of different food categories with similar trends
  • Products of different food categories with different trends

The three first categories can be intesresting to investigate. We will do so in the following graphics

Products of similar food categories with similar trends

We can observe that some categories have the same trends. It is especially visible for the group Meat 1. Due to their similar constraints, meats share similar trends. We can observe especially a similar growth in meats prices between 2014 and 2016.

The group Carrots-Onions is also interesting: we can observe a similar periodicity in the variation of these two products

Products of similar food categories with different trends

For the group hot drinks we can clearly see two different trends for the coffees and the tea. At the start, we can clearly see a decrease in the price of coffees prices up until 2022 and then a stronger increase from 2008 to 2012. Tea on the contrary, even if it follows an attenuated version of the trend of coffee. We can also see this different trends for some fruits. The orange and apples follow a similar increasing with regular spikes pattern, whereas the bananas have only experienced a growth in price after 2008 but keep globally a stable price

Products of different food categories with similar trends

But we can also find products that share common trends even if the do not belong to the same product categories. We can see here two examples where the paper towel has similar trends as the baby food and where the eggs, bread, tomatoe juice and toothpaste share common trends
